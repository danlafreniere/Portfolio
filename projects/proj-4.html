Over the summer of 2015 I developed a series of websites for faculties across Queen's University using Drupal. I maintained regular contact with clients in order to ensure faculty-specific needs were being met. Each project required adherance to Queen's University styling guidelines in order to maintain institutional branding while simultaneously developing a website unique to each faculty. I was able to develop subsequent freelance contracts with each of my clients as a result of my superior attention to detail and ability to develop strong rapport with these individuals. Prior to my departure, I trained a faculty member from each department in order to ensure that the site could be adequately maintained independently.

Queen's Visual Identity

Queen's University is an institution that prides itself on tradition and excellence. In order to help convey these traits, strict visual guidelines must be followed for all content associated with the institution's brand. As a developer on the ITS team, I was tasked with providing clients with a site meeting the functional requirements of their respective faculties while adhering to these visual identity standards. It was an essential experience to learn about company branding and helping client's tailor their needs within the restrictions of a company's brand.
