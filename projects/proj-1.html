Hypertension contributes to a variety of severe health conditions such as heart disease, stroke, and renal failure. Combined with a high prevalence, the disease creates enormous amounts of strain for healthcare practitioners and patients alike. Using data associated with hypertension's contributing factors, I utilized machine learning techniques to predict presence of the disease in a very large sample of patients.

This research is currently being published in the 2016 IEEE Symposium Series in Computational Intelligence conference. An official publication is expected shortly.

Tools

This neural network was not created from scratch. If you want to learn how to do that though, check out the backpropagation post on my blog!

To save some time and sanity, I used a couple of awesome toolboxes:

Microsoft Azure Machine Learning

This toolbox is a beast. It allows you to preprocess all of your data, run R-scripts, test, train, and validate your entire project using a series of interconnected drag-and-drop modules. It's the lego of neural networks and pretty much idiot-proof.

Matlab Neural Network Toolbox

While not as intuitive as the Microsoft toolbox, Matlab still holds its own as a statistical powerhouse. A little more legwork is required in terms of the data preprocessing but scripts can be exported for later use which is a huge plus (to save time for any poor souls that happen to take over the project).
